# Sample Questions & Answers

This document provides sample queries and expected responses from the Mutual Fund FAQ Bot.

## Sample Queries

### 1. Give a description of HDFC Large and Mid Cap Mutual Fund

**Expected Answer:**
HDFC Large and Mid Cap Fund is an open-ended equity scheme that invests in both large-cap and mid-cap stocks. The fund aims to provide long-term capital appreciation by investing in a diversified portfolio of equity and equity-related instruments. It follows a blend of large-cap stability and mid-cap growth potential.

### 2. What is the meaning of Flexi Cap?

**Expected Answer:**
Flexi Cap is a mutual fund category where the fund manager has the flexibility to invest across market capitalizations - large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks - without any restrictions. The fund can dynamically allocate between these segments based on market conditions and opportunities. HDFC Flexi Cap Fund can invest across all market caps with a minimum of 65% in equities.

### 3. Expense ratio of HDFC ELSS Fund?

**Expected Answer:**
The expense ratio for HDFC ELSS Tax Saver Fund varies between Direct and Regular plans. For Direct Plan, the expense ratio is typically lower (around 0.5-0.8% p.a.), while Regular Plan has a higher expense ratio (around 1.5-2.0% p.a.) due to distributor commissions. Please check the latest scheme document for current expense ratios as they may change periodically.

### 4. What is HDFC ELSS Fund lock-in period?

**Expected Answer:**
HDFC ELSS Tax Saver Fund has a mandatory lock-in period of 3 years from the date of investment. This is a regulatory requirement for all ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) funds. During this period, investors cannot redeem their units. The lock-in period applies to each investment separately, so SIP investments have individual 3-year lock-ins from each SIP date.

**Source Links:**
- [AMFI Scheme Document](https://portal.amfiindia.com/spages/861.pdf)

### 6. What is the exit load of HDFC Large and Mid Cap Mutual Fund?

**Expected Answer:**
HDFC Large and Mid Cap Fund typically charges an exit load if units are redeemed within a specified period (usually 1 year from the date of investment). The exit load is generally 1% of the applicable NAV if redeemed within 365 days. No exit load is charged if units are held for more than the specified period. Please check the latest scheme document for current exit load structure.

### 8. How to download capital-gains statements?

**Expected Answer:**
To download capital gains statements for your mutual fund investments:

1. **Through AMC Website**: Log in to your account on the fund house website (e.g., HDFC Mutual Fund), navigate to the statements section, and download capital gains statements.

2. **Through Distributor Platform**: If you invested through platforms like Groww, Zerodha, etc., log in to your account and access the tax statements or capital gains section.

3. **Through Registrar**: Contact the registrar (like CAMS or KFintech) with your folio number to request capital gains statements.

4. **Consolidated Statement**: You can also download consolidated statements from the AMFI website or registrar portals that show all your mutual fund holdings.

**Source Links:**
- [AMFI Portal](https://portal.amfiindia.com/)
- Fund house websites and distributor platforms
